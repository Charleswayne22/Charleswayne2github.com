STEPS TO REGISTER YOUR PROPERTY AND OBTAIN A CERTIFICATE OF OCCUPANCY IN PLATEAU STATE

STEP 1: OBTAIN AND FILL STATORY RIGHT OF OCCUPANCY FORM AT:

THE CUSTOMER SERVICE UNIT
MINISTRY OF LANDS, SURVEY AND TOWN PLANNING,
No 2 J.D GOMWALK BOULEVARD,
JOS, PLATEAU STATE.


OR VISIT PLAGIS TO DOWNLOAD THE FORM.
You can download the fees from PLAGIS

STEP 2: Lodge Applications - takes between 0 to 5 days

STEP 3: Payment of processing fees - takes 3 days

STEP 4: Survey Checks and confirmations - takes 1 day

STEP 5: Town Planning Analysis and Approvals - takes 2 days

STEP 6: Review of Applications and Grant of Provisional Rights - takes 3 days

STEP 7: Acceptance of Rights and Payment of Ground Rent - takes 3 days

STEP 8: Preparation and review of Title Deeds Print - takes 2 days

STEP 9: Generation and Approval of C of O - takes 5 days

STEP 10: Registration and Delivery of C of O - takes between 0 t0 5 days

Jos City

Plateau State is the twelfth largest state of Nigeria, and is roughly located in the center of the country. It is geographically unique in Nigeria because its boundaries totally surround the Jos Plateau, having the Jos Plateau totally in its central and northern part. Its capital is Jos. Plateau State is celebrated as "The Home of Peace and Tourism". Plateau State gets its name from the Jos Plateau. It has a population of around 3.5 million people.
